About WAJAAIS

WAJAAIS is a peer-reviewed open access journal devoted to the intersection of Artificial Intelligence andAgroecology across West Africa. We welcome original research, reviews, and applied case studies combining data science and sustainable agriculture.

  • Publisher: Faculty of Applied Sciences, Alioune Diop University of Bambey (UADB), Senegal
  • Frequency: Biannual
  • Languages: English & French
  • License: CC BY 4.0 (Open Access)

Aims & Scope

WAJAAIS publishes work on AI/ML methods, remote sensing, IoT/Edge-AI, agroecological modelling, climate resilience, and data￾driven solutions for sustainable agriculture in West Africa.

  • Smart agriculture & precision farming
    • Development and application of AI/ML for optimizing agricultural yields.
    • Decision support systems for the management of crops, water, nutrients, and pests.
  • Agroecological modeling & climate adaptation
    • Use of AI-based models to simulate, predict, and improve West African agroecological systems.
    • Climate change adaptation and mitigation strategies in farming practices.
  • Remote sensing & satellite-derived indicators
    • Extraction and analysis of satellite and airborne data for crop health monitoring, soil mapping, and drought assessment.
    • Development of AI-based indicators from remote sensing imagery for rapid intervention.
  • Edge AI, IoT, sensor networks for farming
    • Design and deployment of Internet of Things (IoT) solutions and sensor networks for real-time agricultural data collection.
    • Implementation of Edge AI for local and rapid data processing on the farm.
  • Data-driven policy & socio-technical studies
    • Research on the socio-economic impact and policy implications of adopting AI/ML technologies in West African agriculture.
    • Studies assessing the acceptability, equity, and accessibility of data-driven solutions by smallholder farmers and rural communities.

WAJAAIS strongly encourages contributions that demonstrate concrete applicability and a measurable impact on food security and environmental sustainability in West Africa.
